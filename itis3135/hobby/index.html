What is Swimming:

Swimming is a sport that has been around since 2500 BC, in which your goal is to increase your speed in different strokes, over a long or short distance. There are four strokes in which swimmers compete in at swim meets: freestyle, backstroke, breaststroke, and butterfly. Swimmers can also compete in relays: Medley Relay which combines the four strokes and the Freestyle Relay which only highlights freestyle. Michael Phelps, a swimming legend is the face of the sport, achieving the record in Olympic medals of 28, 23 of them gold!

The Four Strokes:

  • Freestyle: The fastest and most commonly used stroke, freestyle involves alternating arm movements and a flutter kick. Swimmers breathe to the side while maintaining a streamlined body position for efficiency.
  • Backstroke: Swum on the back, this stroke features alternating arm movements and a flutter kick. It is the only competitive stroke where swimmers start in the water and rely on the backstroke flags for turns.
  • Breaststroke: A slower but highly technical stroke, breaststroke requires simultaneous arm movements and a frog-like kick. Timing is key, as swimmers must glide efficiently to maximize speed.
  • Butterfly: Known for its power and difficulty, butterfly features a simultaneous arm movement and a dolphin kick. It requires strong rhythm and endurance, making it one of the most physically demanding strokes.

When to Swim?

Outdoor:

Outdoor swimming is primarily the form of swimming I trained and competed in. I much prefer the outdoors when it comes to swimming, as nothing beats being in the water on a sunny day. Although there are limitations to outdoor swimming, one of them being that it is seasonal. Outdoor pools in NC are typically only open in the summer, leaving use of outdoor pools more limited. Another limitation is lane use, in which pools often have restrictions for swimming as pools can get crowded with families on a hot summer day.

Indoor:

For indoor swimming, swimming can be done year round using indoor pools, no limitations for seasonal effects. Although I do prefer outdoor swimming, I do enjoy swimming laps in an indoor facility as well. For serious swimmers it is a necessity to swim year round and in order to do that swimmers must find an indoor pool to train at. If you are a begginner and are only open to swimming on a nice summer day, indoor swimming may not be for you. Typically indoor swimming is for more advanced swimmers, but most facilities do offer swimming lessons for those less experienced. Their usually are membership fees to join an indoor pool, but if you are looking to train your swimming technique all year round I would highly reccomend it.

How to Start Swimming

Jump Into the Water:

The best way to get involved in the sport of swimming is to simply start. Jump into the water, start by swimming laps and improve from there. Everybody starts at a different fitness level, some people naturally can swim more laps at a faster pace, but it dosen't matter where you start as long as you are able to consistently train. If you are struggling to get going you might need assistance from a swim coach. Lessons are available all over and this can help jumpstart your progress. Online resources are also available to help guide your technique on different strokes and can offer training programs.

You Don't Need to Swim Alone:

Starting off into the world of swimming can be hard, but there is a big swimming community out there to explore. Joining lessons at your local facility can help improve your morale on swimming as working out with your peers can be more fun and engaging. There are also local swim teams you can join, some catering to begginners and some catering to more competitive swimmers. It is completely fine to swim on your own, but I would highly reccomend to try swimming with others, especially if you are new to the sport and can get some helpful guidance.
